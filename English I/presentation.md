## **Slide 2: Meaning of Child Labour**

* Child labour is when children are involved in work that harms their **physical, mental, or emotional development**.
* It includes:

  * Long working hours
  * Very low wages
  * Unsafe and unhealthy work environments
* If a child’s work **stops them from attending school**, it is child labour.

---

## **Slide 3: Why This Topic Is Important**

* Child labour affects **millions** of children in Bangladesh.
* It **destroys childhood**, blocks education, and harms the country’s future workforce.
* Understanding this issue helps us find ways to protect children.

---

## **Slide 4: Current Facts and Statistics**

* According to the **Labour Force Survey 2022**:

  * **3.5 million** children (ages 5–17) are working.
  * More than **1 million** work in dangerous or hazardous jobs.
* Sectors include: factories, shops, farming, brickfields, transport, domestic work.

# **Member 2 – Nafis: Cause 1 – Poverty and Social Pressure**

## **Slide 1: Poverty – The Main Reason**

* Many Bangladeshi families cannot meet basic needs like food, rent, medicine.
* Children become “extra earners” to support their families.
* A child’s wage may help the family survive.

---

## **Slide 2: Real Story Example**

* Example: A 12-year-old boy in Dhaka works **12 hours daily** at a tea stall.
* Despite long hours, he earns very little.
* He works because his parents depend on him financially.

---

## **Slide 3: Social and Cultural Pressure**

* In rural areas, it is common for children to help in farming from a young age.
* Some parents believe **earning is more important than schooling**.
* Children are encouraged to “learn work early.”

---

## **Slide 4: The Informal Job Market**

* Bangladesh has a large informal economy:

  * Tea stalls
  * Street shops
  * Small factories
  * Home-based work
* These workplaces have **no contracts, no safety rules**, and children are easily exploited.

---

# **Member 3 – Debapriya: Cause 2 – Lack of Access to Education**

## **Slide 1: Education Is Not Fully Free**

Hidden costs force many children to drop out:

* Books and notebooks
* Uniforms
* Exam fees
* Transportation
* Private tutoring

Poor families cannot afford these expenses.

---

## **Slide 2: School Attendance Is Low**

* Only **28–29%** of working children attend school.
* Many leave school permanently once they start working.

---

## **Slide 3: Parental Mindset**

* Uneducated parents may not value formal schooling.
* They believe school cannot guarantee income.
* So they send children to work early.

---

## **Slide 4: Distance & Quality Issues**

* Some rural schools are far away.
* Many schools have **poor facilities, crowded classrooms, or untrained teachers**.
* Children choose work over attending ineffective schools.

---

# **Member 4 – Parthee: Effects of Child Labour**

## **Slide 1: Loss of Education**

* Working children miss classes or drop out.
* They fail to develop skills and literacy.
* This limits future job opportunities and income.

---

## **Slide 2: Health Problems**

Children face physical dangers in risky jobs such as brickfields, factories, metal workshops:

* Cuts and injuries
* Breathing problems from dust/chemicals
* Back pain from heavy lifting
* Malnutrition due to long work hours

---

## **Slide 3: Emotional Stress**

* Stress, anxiety, and exhaustion are common.
* No time for rest, play, or childhood experiences.
* Some children experience verbal or physical abuse.

---

## **Slide 4: Generational Poverty Cycle**

* Children with no education become low-income adults.
* Their children also go to work instead of school.
* Poverty continues generation after generation.

---

# **Member 5 – Tajbiha: Solutions**

## **Slide 1: Government Actions**

* Strictly enforce child labour laws.
* Increase inspections in factories and workshops.
* Punish employers who illegally hire children.

---

## **Slide 2: Improve Education Access**

Provide **truly free** education by ensuring:

* Free textbooks and uniforms
* Free or low-cost transport
* Mid-day meals in school
* Scholarships for poor students

---

## **Slide 3: Support for Families**

Help poor families so children don’t have to work:

* Cash support
* Food aid
* Skill development programs for parents
* Job placement programs

---

## **Slide 4: NGO & Community Role**

NGOs can help by:

* Rescuing children from hazardous jobs
* Offering informal schooling
* Raising awareness among parents

Communities should encourage school attendance.

---

# **Member 6 – Sabbir: Conclusion**

## **Slide 1: Summary of Key Points**

* **Causes:** Poverty and lack of education.
* **Effects:** Health problems, loss of schooling, and continuation of poverty.
* **Solutions:** Government action, improved education, family support, NGOs.

---

## **Slide 2: Hope for the Future**

* Bangladesh is making progress in reducing child labour.
* With stronger efforts, more children can return to school.
* Community and government together can break the child labour cycle.
