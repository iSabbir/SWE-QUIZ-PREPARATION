**Paragraph A:** Plastic has become an inseparable part of modern life. Since plastic is not biodegradable, it does not decompose naturally. Instead, it persists in the environment for hundreds of years, accumulating in landfills and natural habitats at an alarming rate.

**Paragraph B:** The most visible impact of this pollution is found in our oceans. **Paragraph C:** Marine wildlife suffers immensely from this invasion. Animals such as sea turtles often mistake plastic bags for jellyfish, their primary food source. **Paragraph E:** Addressing this global crisis requires a multi-faceted approach. Governments are beginning to implement bans on single-use plastics like straws and bags. **Malala: A Voice for Education**

Malala Yousafzai was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, a town in the Swat Valley of Pakistan. Her father, Ziauddin, was a teacher who ran a girls' school and believed strongly that women should have the same rights as men. When Malala was ten years old, the Taliban took control of the Swat Valley and banned girls from attending school.

Despite the danger, Malala refused to stay silent. In early 2009, she began writing a blog for the BBC under a pseudonym (a fake name), describing her life under Taliban rule and her desire to go to school. Her voice grew louder, and she began appearing on television to advocate for female education. This made her a target. On October 9, 2012, while riding the bus home from school, a gunman boarded the vehicle and shot her.

Malala survived the attack but was in critical condition. She was flown to Birmingham, England, for specialized medical treatment. Her recovery was miraculous. Instead of retreating into fear, she used her global platform to continue her fight. On her 16th birthday, she gave a famous speech at the United Nations, declaring that "One child, one teacher, one book, and one pen can change the world."

In 2014, at the age of 17, Malala became the youngest-ever recipient of the Nobel Peace Prize. She continues to work today through the Malala Fund, an organization dedicated to ensuring every girl around the world has access to twelve years of free, safe, and quality education.
