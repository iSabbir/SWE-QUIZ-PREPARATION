## 1. History of the ICT Industry in Bangladesh (≤200 words)

Bangladesh has nearly six decades of experience with computer technology. The journey began in **1964** when the first *second-generation mainframe computer* was installed at **Dhaka University**. Soon after, banks and industrial organizations started using computers mainly for accounting and data processing.

A major milestone in ICT education occurred in **1982** with the establishment of a computer center at **BUET**, which later evolved into the **Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE)** and played a crucial role in producing skilled IT professionals.

In **1987**, **Mainul Islam** demonstrated Bangla typing on an Apple Macintosh, making Bangla writing on computers possible and increasing office-level adoption. The arrival of the **internet in 1996** opened doors for software export and multimedia development.

Several institutions supported sectoral growth, including **BCS (1979)**, **National Computer Committee (1983)** later becoming **BCC**, and **BASIS (1997)**. Infrastructure development expanded with the **SEA-ME-WE 4 submarine cable (2006)**, followed by initiatives such as **BHTPA (2010)**, **STP (2015)**, and **a2i**, supporting the vision of *Digital Bangladesh*.

## 2. Key Objectives of ICT Policy 2018 in Bangladesh (≤200 words)

The **National ICT Policy 2018** outlines an eight-objective framework aimed at transforming Bangladesh into a knowledge-based digital economy. These objectives guide policy actions across governance, economy, and society.

### The Eight Key Objectives:
1. **Digital Government** – Ensuring efficient, transparent, and citizen-centric public services  
2. **Digital Security** – Strengthening cybersecurity and data protection  
3. **Social Equity & Universal Access** – Reducing the digital divide  
4. **Education, Research & Innovation** – Promoting ICT-based learning and R&D  
5. **Skill Development & Employment Generation** – Creating a skilled digital workforce  
6. **Strengthening Domestic Capability** – Enhancing local ICT industry competitiveness  
7. **Environment, Climate & Disaster Management** – Using ICT for resilience and sustainability  
8. **Enhancing Productivity** – Improving efficiency across all economic sectors  

These objectives are presented under the structure **“National ICT Policy 2018 (8 Objectives & 55 Themes)”**, forming the foundation for Digital Bangladesh and Smart Bangladesh initiatives.

## 3. Strengths and Weaknesses of the ICT Industry in Bangladesh (≤200 words)

### Strengths / Prospects
A major strength of Bangladesh’s ICT industry is its **young, educated, and cost-competitive workforce**. Although the sector is still relatively small, it is growing rapidly, particularly in **software and IT service exports**. BASIS projects increasing GDP contribution and workforce expansion in coming years. Export earnings from software and IT services have crossed **USD 1 billion**, and **hi-tech parks** such as Kaliakoir are expected to generate large-scale employment.

### Weaknesses / Problems
Despite growth, the sector faces significant challenges. **Infrastructure and power shortages**, along with unreliable electricity, affect productivity. **Limited internet speed and bandwidth** remain major constraints. The industry also lacks capacity for **large, long-term projects**. There is a noticeable **gap between graduate skills and industry needs**, particularly in soft skills. Insufficient investment and **political instability** further hinder sustainable growth.
